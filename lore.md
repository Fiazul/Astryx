# Astryx — Lore

> The world, the fleet, and the people of *Astryx*. This is the in-universe codex:
> who flies, what they fly, and what waits out in the dark.

## The world
Space is quiet, and mostly empty, and that is exactly the point. *Astryx* is the long
fall outward from home — no war to win, no menus to clear, nothing to collect but
distance. You leave **Earth**, you point the nose at a glowing dot on the black, and
you go. The dot grows. It resolves. It becomes a world. Then you pick another dot.

Everything is measured from **Sol**, the still center the charts are drawn around.
Far enough out and the old numbers stop meaning anything; pilots learn to read the
sky in light-years and trust the glow over the gauge.

## Rook
**Rook** is the pilot. He flies because the alternative is standing still, and he was
never any good at standing still. He doesn't say much. He launches from Earth in
whatever hull the station hands him and lets the quiet do the rest.

## The fleet
The fleet is *hers* — a line of hulls Rook can swap into at the station, each with her
own temper and her own way of eating distance. You don't own a ship in *Astryx*. You
borrow one, and she decides how the trip goes.

- **Lyra** — the starter. The hull they hand you first, the one you launch in. Honest,
  unfussy, forgiving of a clumsy hand on the stick. Everyone's first sky was Lyra's.

- **Stella** — the original. The OG: balanced, dependable, the ride that never lets
  you down and never shows off about it. The name once belonged to Lyra and was set
  free; now it's Stella's alone, and it fits her.

- **Raptor** — the dangerous one. She runs two faces and you flip between them on a
  switch: **Combat**, where she becomes a wall of fire, and **Warp**, where she tears
  a hole in the distance and crosses it before the light catches up. Her booster burns
  purple — you know her by the trail.

- **Vela** — the sleek FTL cruiser, built for one thing: speed. Her warp drive winds
  up slow and then *blazes*, crossing a whole system on a single charge. Her booster
  burns gold. Pilots talk about Vela the way they talk about weather they barely
  survived.

## Vortex
Out past the safe lanes, in the **Alien Zone**, the regular hostiles drift — and among
them, the thing they're afraid of. **Vortex** is an alien hull grown monstrous, scaled
up far past anything a person should fly, lit with a red-hot menace that the dark
can't swallow. He is the boss of the deep: when his name is on the sky, the whole
sky knows it.

## The way of flying
Flying in *Astryx* has weight to it. You aim with the eye, you lean on the throttle,
and the hull banks into her turns like she means them. Push hard and she boosts;
ease off and she settles. The galaxy is too big to take all at once, so the good
pilots learn its rhythms:

- **Warp / FTL** — the long-haul gift. Vela and Raptor-in-Warp spool their drives up
  toward light-year cruise and then back down. While she's screaming across the void,
  the guns go quiet — there's no fighting at that speed, only arriving.

- **Free-look** — take your eye off the road. Hold the look and the view drifts off
  the tail to drink in the sky; let go and it snaps back behind her where it belongs.

- **The approach** — the closer you fall toward a world, the gentler she gets, easing
  down so you can really *see* it. Point away and she's free again, and the dot
  shrinks back into the dark behind you.

## Earth, and the dots
You start at **Earth**, the launch point, the place the whole map remembers as home.
From there it's all dots — pinpricks of glow that are stars, planets, the rare strange
light that turns out to be something else. None of them are solid little worlds you can
land on and walk. They are *glow*: emissive, burning, growing as you close, the way a
real far thing looks when it finally gets near. That's the whole feeling of *Astryx* —
the dot becomes a world, and then you go find the next one.
